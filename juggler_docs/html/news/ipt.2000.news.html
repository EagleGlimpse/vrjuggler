June 19-20, 2000
 VR Juggler Shows off at IPT2000

   
 

This year's Immersive Projection Technology Workshop (IPT 2000) unveiled VRAC's new 6 sided cave-like device named the C6. To show off this new system, applications written on top of the Juggler virtual platform (about 22 total) were demonstrated for the 300+ attendees. Everything was a success: framerate was high, crashes were non-existent... . . . .

more info...

. . . ...There were very few problems the only one being instability problems with our new wireless Motionstar tracking system. As it turned out, it was a hardware bug and still suffers from occasional dropouts.

Of special note, we ran every application within one reconfigurable application (an at-runtime reconfigurable environment). The architechture of Juggler allows you to do special things like switching and configuration of applications at runtime. This demonstrates yet another facet of the Juggler Platform's usefulness over your typical VR device driver library.

Also at the conference, two of our own Juggler team presented papers on two juggler features: Run-time reconfiguration by Allen Bierbaum, and performance monitoring by Chris Just.
